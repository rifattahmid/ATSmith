# Resume Extended Context Example

## Purpose

This file shows the recommended structure for `resume.extended.md`.

Use `resume.source.md` for strict facts. Use `resume.extended.md` for careful adjacent language that is defensible from those facts but should not be treated as a direct claim on its own.

Copy this file into a country or category template folder as `resume.extended.md`, then replace the synthetic examples with your own source-backed context.

## Rules

- Use this file only alongside `resume.source.md`.
- Do not invent direct experience.
- Do not turn exposure into ownership.
- Prefer support verbs when the source does not prove ownership: supported, developed, built, analysed, modelled, maintained, processed, reconciled, designed, conducted, performed, leveraged, contributed to.
- Avoid ownership verbs unless the source clearly supports them: owned, led, administered, negotiated, submitted, approved, managed, was responsible for.
- Keep risky phrases in the boundaries section.
- Treat each section as a permission map, not as final resume bullet text.

## Reference Section Format

Use this pattern when creating extended files for your own countries or categories:

### Theme Name

**Source basis:** Factual bullets, roles, tools, metrics, coursework, or certifications from `resume.source.md` that support the mapping.

**Supported transferable language:**

- Adjacent ATS/recruiter keywords that are defensible from the source facts.
- Prefer exposure/support language when ownership is not directly supported.

**Example safe usage:**

- One concise phrase or bullet fragment showing how the mapping can be used without inventing experience.

**Boundaries:**

- Direct claims, ownership verbs, regulated responsibilities, or seniority that should not be implied unless added to `resume.source.md`.

## Supported Transferable Language

### FP&A, Budgeting & Rolling Forecasts

**Source basis:** Built budget and forecast models at Example Infrastructure Co; supported monthly performance tracking, variance analysis, and dashboard reporting.

**Supported transferable language:** FP&A support, budgeting, forecasting, rolling forecast, budget performance tracking, variance analysis, monthly reporting, forecast model development, cost phasing, planning cycle support.

**Example safe usage:** Supported FP&A planning cycles through rolling forecast models, budget performance tracking, and variance analysis.

**Boundaries:** Do not imply ownership of the full budget process, budget approval, or final planning governance.

### Revenue Modelling & Commercial Analysis

**Source basis:** Developed a contract-driven revenue scenario model for Example Infrastructure Co using customer-level assumptions, pricing inputs, and capacity constraints.

**Supported transferable language:** revenue modelling, commercial finance analysis, pricing analysis support, contract-driven revenue analysis, customer-level revenue analysis, capacity allocation analysis, growth scenario analysis.

**Example safe usage:** Modelled contract-driven revenue scenarios using pricing inputs, customer-level assumptions, and capacity constraints.

**Boundaries:** Do not claim pricing strategy ownership, customer negotiation, contract negotiation, or commercial approval authority.

### CapEx, OpEx & Project Finance Support

**Source basis:** Analysed project cost data across capital and operating expenditure categories; supported forecast updates and project performance reporting.

**Supported transferable language:** CapEx analysis, OpEx analysis, project finance support, capital project reporting, cost control support, project cost analysis, cost visibility, expenditure forecasting.

**Example safe usage:** Conducted CapEx and OpEx analysis to support forecasting, cost control, and project performance tracking.

**Boundaries:** Do not claim project delivery ownership, procurement authority, capital approval authority, or ownership of project budgets.

### Accounting, Reconciliations & Controls

**Source basis:** Processed journals, reconciled ledger and sub-ledger data, supported audit readiness checks, and reviewed financial data quality.

**Supported transferable language:** journal processing, month-end support, general ledger reconciliation, sub-ledger reconciliation, audit readiness, data integrity checks, control support, financial accounting support.

**Example safe usage:** Supported month-end close through journal processing, reconciliations, and audit-ready financial data checks.

**Boundaries:** Do not claim statutory reporting ownership, audit sign-off, accounting policy ownership, or final control ownership.

### Dashboarding, Analytics & Management Reporting

**Source basis:** Designed Excel and BI dashboards to monitor project, invoice, supplier, employee, and financial data.

**Supported transferable language:** dashboard reporting, management reporting, KPI reporting, financial analytics, exception monitoring, trend analysis, reporting automation, data visualisation.

**Example safe usage:** Built dashboard reporting to monitor financial trends, operational exceptions, and management reporting inputs.

**Boundaries:** Do not claim enterprise BI ownership, executive reporting ownership, or system administration unless supported by `resume.source.md`.

### Valuation, Scenario Analysis & Investment Support

**Source basis:** Built valuation-style models, stress-tested assumptions, and analysed scenario impacts using spreadsheet and statistical tools.

**Supported transferable language:** valuation support, DCF-style modelling, scenario analysis, sensitivity analysis, stress testing, investment analysis support, assumption testing, returns analysis support.

**Example safe usage:** Developed valuation-style scenario models to test assumptions, forecast impacts, and support investment analysis.

**Boundaries:** Do not claim formal investment recommendation authority, transaction execution, board advisory ownership, or final valuation sign-off.

## Do Not Claim Unless Added To resume.source.md

- Owned the budget process.
- Approved forecasts or budgets.
- Negotiated customer contracts.
- Administered debt facilities or statutory submissions.
- Signed off audit, tax, or regulatory reports.
- Led investment committees or board presentations.
- Made final investment recommendations.
- Owned enterprise systems or acted as system administrator.
